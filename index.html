Our Firm

We are one of the most respected and longest tenured firms in the region. We have purposely remained at a size specifically designed to deliver the depth, breadth, and quality of legal services you’d expect from a large national law firm, but delivered in the personalized, attentive manner you’d expect from a cutting edge boutique law firm. Bankruptcy & Creditor's Rights

The Cors & Bassett attorneys represent secured and unsecured lenders in various bankruptcy-related matters. We also have extensive experience with workouts and complex restructurings of businesses. Our work with creditors includes assistance with collections and liquidations.

The C&B attorneys also represent clients in various bankruptcy proceedings in U.S. bankruptcy courts. We have successfully represented clients in defending against preference and other avoidance claims, as well as pursuing client claims through adversary proceedings to determine non-dischargeability. We have also represented financial institutions in defending against various lender liability claims. C&B attorneys regularly provide our business clients with credit management advice, and our experience and expertise helps our clients avoid pitfalls and enhance their relationships with current and prospective customers.

  • Collection and Liquidation
  • Credit Management
  • Lender Liability
  • Workouts, Reorganizations

Employee Benefits

The complex and sometimes murky laws and regulations governing employee benefit and executive compensation plans require precise drafting, technical know-how, and a future-oriented approach. The Cors & Bassett employee benefit attorneys possess all of these traits. Knowing the law is not enough – it must be coupled with an understanding of our clients’ businesses and how the application of the law serves those businesses and employee benefit plans. Services provided by C&B employee benefit attorneys include:

  • Assessment of business needs, compensation philosophy, and financial constraints.
  • Drafting and assistance with maintenance of tax-qualified retirement plans, including 401(k) and pension plans.
  • Negotiation and drafting of executive compensation and incentive plans.
  • Drafting employee stock option plans, employee stock ownership plans (ESOPs), and other equity-based incentive compensation arrangements.
  • Counseling regarding benefit plan terminations and the reversion of pension plan assets.
  • Identification of employee benefit and executive compensation issues in business transactions such as mergers, acquisitions, and divestures.
  • Negotiation and drafting of supplemental retirement and other non-qualified deferred compensation plans, including funding arrangements through insurance plans and trusts.
  • Drafting Section 125 employee cafeteria plans.
  • Assistance with COBRA health care continuation issues and HIPAA compliance.
  • Advising plan sponsors, administrators, and trustees regarding ERISA fiduciary standards and reporting and disclosure requirements.
  • Litigation of ERISA disputes.
  • Compliance with all federal and state agency reporting requirements (IRS, Department of Labor, SEC, and Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation).
  • Negotiation and drafting of early retirement and severance agreements.
  • Litigation involving multi-employer benefit plans.

Franchising

The C&B attorneys in our franchising practice group represent both franchisees and franchisors as well as dealers, distributors, and manufacturers involved in dealership networks. C&B attorneys draw on their extensive experience to help clients on either side of the franchise or dealership relationship navigate all aspects of what is often a complex and difficult relationship, including the drafting, review, and negotiation of franchise disclosure documents and franchise agreements, dealership and distributorship agreements, assistance with related trademark issues, and expertise regarding the complex web of state franchising, dealership, and distribution law.

C&B attorneys represent several locally-based franchisors and handle all aspects of compliance with Federal and state franchise law as well as those franchisors’ relationships with their respective franchisees and developers. We also represent franchisees of all sizes – from multi-unit franchisees with territories covering multiple states to single-unit start-ups-with respect to the review of franchise disclosure documents and the negotiation of franchise and related agreements.

C&B attorneys also represent several local automobile dealers in their relationships with both manufacturers and consumers. These services include:

  • Litigation of claims by consumers regarding warranty claims & CSPA claims.
  • Resolving disputes with manufacturers regarding warranty and sales audits, sales incentive disputes, protesting the establishment of new dealers, and disputes regarding warranty payments.
  • Litigation of claims in front of the Ohio Motor Vehicle Dealers Board.

C&B attorneys also represent one of the country’s largest trailer manufacturers and assist that manufacturer with:

  • Consumer disputes.
  • Drafting of consumer warranties and other dealer policies.
  • Drafting of dealer agreements.
  • The enforcement of dealer agreements.
  • All other agreements relating to the dealer relationship including warranty reimbursement and dealer terminations.

Our attorneys also assist manufacturers with trademark issues and financing issues.

Our attorneys also guide our clients in protecting their creative works such as art, music, literature and computer programs. We search and register copyrights and advise our clients about copyright protection and infringement.

We are also experienced in drafting and negotiating software licenses, whether our clients are the software owners or the software users.

Our intellectual property attorneys advise clients in all industries about how to develop, establish, market, and protect their brands and ideas. Labor & Employment

Today, employers must operate in a highly regulated and litigious business climate. C&B prides itself on helping employers minimize the legal risks involved in making employment decisions without compromising business needs. Our employment law attorneys develop strong relationships with our clients' Human Resource professionals, so that assistance with any employment law issue is only a telephone call or email away. Because of the depth of C&B's labor and employment team, we are able to provide assistance on even the most urgent of matters.

Services offered by our labor and employment attorneys include:

  • Designing policies and practices that minimize the risk of successful claims of discriminatory, equal pay violations, breach of employment contract, as well as wrongful, or retaliatory discharge.
  • Keeping clients abreast of changes in federal and state employment laws.
  • Evaluating vulnerabilities through review of personnel policies and employee handbooks.
  • Assisting employers in conducting internal investigations.
  • Drafting employment agreements, non-competition agreements, confidentiality agreements and policies, invention agreements, and other documents, which protect clients’ trade secrets and other confidential information.
  • Assisting clients with planning and implementing individual terminations and group reductions in force in compliance with federal and state WARN statutes, and minimizing the risk of discrimination claims by drafting appropriate severance agreements and communications to affected employees.
  • Assisting clients with implementing proactive strategies for remaining union free and responding to first signs of card signing activity.
  • Advising clients regarding compliance with Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA) regulations and representing clients accused of OSHA violations.

C&B also has decades of experience in litigating employment issues, including: wrongful discharge cases and discrimination cases; breaches of employment, non-competition, and non-disclosure agreements; defending unemployment benefits and workers' compensation claims; and representing employers before the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, Occupational Safety and Health Administration, and other federal and state agencies.

The firm also has a long, rich history of assisting clients with traditional labor law issues, a rare find in the Greater Cincinnati legal community. Our labor and employment attorneys have assisted countless clients in the following:

  • Negotiation and administration of collective bargaining agreements.
  • Arbitrating contractual labor disputes.
  • Resisting organizational attempts.
  • Representing clients in proceedings before the National Labor Relations Board.

The firm also advises and assists clients in the planning, development, and implementation of both defined benefit and defined contribution plans. Our experienced attorneys:

  • Draft employee benefit program documents and submit the program for qualification by the Internal Revenue Service and other regulatory agencies.
  • Advise clients regarding the termination of plans that no longer serve the client’s goals.
  • Assist clients in the development of non-qualified plans for key employees.
  • Develop employee benefit plans to fit our clients’ specific needs, while integrating employee benefit matters within the overall tax and business goals.
